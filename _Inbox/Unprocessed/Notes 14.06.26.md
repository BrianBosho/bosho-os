### Delusion, and being unreasonable and reality distortion
>There core idea here is that we live under the impression that reality operates within certain constraints, even though most of those constraints we are the ones that impose it or are imposed upon us by society e.g. the 4 mile run limit. In this case, to be reasonable to work withing the constraints we have accepted. And to be unreasonable or delusional is otherwise. 


A critical thing here is to discover that most of what we belief about ourselves and the world is what shapes the reality we experience. And the results of this is that, we end up having our life within constraints we maybe not even consciously have chose or been aware of. More importantly we fail to notice that beliefs are not truths. They are cognitive tools. 
- Core thing is reality is extremely malleable, and being delusional is OK (Within what we can consider as fundamental constraints)
- Imagination is important as its the way we get out of our environment
- Beliefs are tools not truths. 
- The point of a tool is that it works. Not if it is true. or if it is right. 
- We are driven by thing we have no idea about. and that they are driving us.
- We live within beliefs and ideas that we did not choose and have not idea that we are living by them.

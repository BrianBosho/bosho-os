Your week needs a stable architecture:

## Default workday

**8:00–11:00 — Fedprop**

Same project, same time, every workday. No deciding whether you feel ready.

Each morning must have one defined output:

- results audit and experiment decisions;
    
- rewrite a specific set of tables/results;
    
- run or analyze necessary experiments;
    
- revise the appendix;
    
- integrate and send the draft.
    

Four or five mornings gives you **12–15 hours**. That should be enough to move Fedprop from a frightening abstraction into a reviewable draft, even if it does not finish every possible improvement.

**1:00–4:00 — Second deep-work block**

This is where you rotate the other substantive work:

- Monday: VLM project architecture and task breakdown.
    
- Tuesday: VLM technical work or Fedprop overflow.
    
- Thursday: VLM contribution and team unblocking.
    
- Friday: Collab work or final Fed prop integration.
    

**4:30–6:00/6:30 — Exercise block**
Time to work out or go out. and hang out 

**7:30–9:00/ — Logistics block**

Only one logistics theme per day:

- Portugal decision;
    
- visa expedition;
    
- emails and conversations;
    
- Andrew ID;
    
- scheduling and administration.
    

Do not enter the block with the instruction “handle logistics.” Enter with something bounded:

> Find the decision deadline and draft the questions for Jaime.

or:

> Establish the emergency visa-request procedure and identify the documents needed.

## A workable week

### Monday

- 8:00–11:00: Fedprop audit and exact task list
    
- 1:00–4:00: VLM project plan and delegation
    
- 4:30–6:00: Andrew ID + establish Portugal decision deadline
    

### Tuesday

- 8:00–11:00: Fedprop results rewrite
    
- 1:00–4:00: VLM first technical contribution
    
- 4:30–6:00: Prepare communication and questions for Jaime/current students
    

### Wednesday

Keep your recovery day. At most, do one genuinely urgent administrative action. Do not use it to compensate for Monday and Tuesday.

### Thursday

- 8:00–11:00: Fedprop results/experiments
    
- 1:00–4:00: VLM team-unblocking work
    
- 4:30–6:00: Visa expedition process and school email
    

### Friday

- 8:00–11:00: Fedprop appendix, integration, and draft package
    
- 1:00–4:00: Colab or remaining Fedprop work
    
- 4:30–6:00: Portugal decision synthesis and next actions
    

This gives you:

- **12 hours of protected Fedprop work**
    
- **9 hours available for VLM/Colab**
    
- **6 hours of contained logistics**
    
- **one real recovery day**
    

That is enough to radically change how the landscape feels.

The main rule is:

> **The schedule decides the category; the first five minutes decide the exact output.**

You should not spend the morning deciding whether Fedprop, Portugal, or VLM is more urgent. At 8:00, it is Fedprop because that is what the architecture says. At 4:30, the paper loses the right to address you because logistics now owns the floor.

And after the logistics block, perform a five-minute shutdown:

1. Record what was completed.
    
2. Write the next action.
    
3. Confirm tomorrow’s first output.
    
4. Declare the workday closed.
    

The plan works because it converts the entire mass into recurring containers. You do not need to feel excited about every concern. You only need to arrive at the correct container.

# Altitude Check Protocol

The Altitude Check is a scheduled conversation with someone senior enough to see the landscape.

It is different from:

- advisor meetings
- project meetings
- peer chats
- monthly self-review

Its purpose is to prevent local-optimum thinking.

## Cadence

Year 1: every 4–6 weeks.

Later: every 8–10 weeks once direction stabilizes.

## Possible people

- [[Aashiq]]
- advanced PhD student/postdoc in VLMs/interp/safety
- trusted senior student who understands CMU and the field
- later: [[03_People/Target_Advisors/Virginia Smith]] or other technical advisor

## Pre-meeting memo

Send a short memo with:

1. What I am currently working on.
2. What I think the research question is.
3. What I am unsure about.
4. What progress happened since last check.
5. What decision I need help making.

## Questions to ask

- Is this interesting?
- Is this too vague?
- Is this the right level of ambition?
- Am I missing obvious related work?
- Is this a good use of the next month?
- Who should I talk to?
- What would make this publishable?
- What would make this thesis-relevant?

## Output

Every altitude check must produce:

- one decision
- one correction
- one person/paper/project to follow up on
- one concrete next action

## Hard rule

If I am unsure whether a project is real, I do not solve that alone. I take it to an altitude check.

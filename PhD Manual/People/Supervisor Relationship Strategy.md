# Supervisor Relationship Strategy

## Principle

Do not treat people notes as fantasy org charts.

A person’s note should reflect the actual relationship status, not the relationship I hope will exist.

## Core strategy

The goal is not to impress supervisors with polished certainty. The goal is to build trust through clarity, momentum, honesty, and good judgment.

## Meeting template

Before each meeting:

1. What did I do since last time?
2. What is the current research question?
3. What artifact exists?
4. What am I stuck on?
5. What decision do I need?
6. What will I do before next meeting?

After each meeting:

1. What did they actually say?
2. What did they seem excited by?
3. What did they seem skeptical of?
4. What commitment did I make?
5. What should I send them next?

## Clarify early

If I am confused about expectations, direction, authorship, funding, internships, collaboration boundaries, or advisor roles, clarify early.

## Relationship anti-patterns

- Hiding uncertainty.
- Waiting too long to show work.
- Asking for commitment before showing a concrete project.
- Treating one advisor as the only source of intellectual direction.
- Over-optimizing for approval instead of research truth.

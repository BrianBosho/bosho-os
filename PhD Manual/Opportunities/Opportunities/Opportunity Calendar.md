# Opportunity Calendar

## Purpose

Prevent reactive scrambling. Track opportunities early enough to build relationships, materials, and advisor approval.

## Compatibility rule

Before applying to any paid, full-time, off-campus, or fellowship-like opportunity, check:

- advisor approval
- CMU/FEUP compatibility
- FCT scholarship rules if in Portugal
- OIE/CPT/visa rules if in the U.S.
- IP/conflict-of-interest issues

## Opportunity notes

### MATS

- Strong fit if project can be framed around AI safety, interpretability, evaluations, or alignment-relevant empirical research.
- Needs serious timing/compatibility analysis because of location and full-time commitment.

### Anthropic Fellows

- Strong fit if the VLM self-evaluation project becomes clearly empirical, safety-relevant, and executable.
- Work authorization issue is central.

### SPAR

- Potentially useful as lower-friction mentorship and research loop.
- Watch for projects related to interpretability, evaluations, multimodal safety, or model internals.

### OpenAI Safety Fellowship

- 2026 pilot seems closed, but the research areas overlap with safety evaluation, robustness, and empirically grounded safety research.

### Cambridge AI Safety Hub (MARS / Alignment Fellowship)

- MARS is part-time and team-based (2–4 people with a mentor), which makes it lower-friction than full-time programs.
- Covers compute ($2k+), travel/accommodation for in-person week, and Claude Max for technical streams.
- Mentors include Redwood Research, IAPS, and Google DeepMind — high-signal network access.
- The Alignment Fellowship is an intro course; useful if entering the safety ecosystem formally for the first time.
- Strong cultural fit: Cambridge is one of the leading nodes in the global AI safety ecosystem alongside Berkeley.

### ERA:AI Fellowship

- 10-week fully-funded programme in Cambridge UK.
- Safety + governance framing needed; interpretability angle fits.
- Useful if in a Portugal period — Cambridge is easier to reach from Porto than from Pittsburgh.

### CBAI (Cambridge Boston Alignment Initiative)

- Based between Cambridge (MIT/Harvard) and Boston; summer programme.
- Geographically close to CMU; could work during a Pittsburgh summer period.
- Watch for future cycles.

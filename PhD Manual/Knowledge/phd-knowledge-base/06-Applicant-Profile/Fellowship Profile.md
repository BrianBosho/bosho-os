## Universal Fellowship Narrative

### The Problem I Solve
Vision-language models hallucinate — they describe objects that aren't there, mislocalize spatial relationships, and fail under domain shift. This is not just a benchmark problem; it matters in medical imaging, autonomous vehicles, and scientific instruments. I am developing the scientific foundations to fix it.

### Why I Am Different
1. **I come from an underrepresented context** — Africa is the most underrepresented continent in top-tier AI research, yet has some of the most challenging and understudied AI deployment environments. My research on visual grounding in Kigali traffic scenes is not just fieldwork — it is scientifically revealing failure modes that benchmarks in well-funded Western labs miss.

2. **I bridge theory and practice** — My FedProp work exemplifies this: I identified a missing-feature problem, designed an algorithm with convergence guarantees, and implemented it. A fellowship is not just funding for me; it is infrastructure for a research agenda that requires both.

3. **I produce** — Valedictorian, top of class, first-authored paper under review, teaching assistant, local chair of ACVSS 2025.

## Google PhD Fellowship — Machine Perception

### Pitch
My research on visual grounding and VLM hallucination is fundamentally a **machine perception** problem — understanding what is in an image, where it is, and whether language aligns with reality. I study this across two domains: (1) biomedical imaging where errors have real consequences, and (2) urban mobility in Kigali where domain shift exposes the brittleness of current VFMs.

### Key Evidence
- FedProp paper under review (algorithm + proofs + implementation)
- Cryo-ET pipeline with 3D U-Net and DeepETPicker
- VQA probing of VLM failures in low-resource settings
- Research interests aligned with Pillar 1 (Truly Perceptual Models)

### Why I Need This Fellowship
As an international student from Africa, I need full funding to pursue a PhD at a top-tier institution. This fellowship would cover tuition and living expenses, enabling me to focus entirely on research. It would also signal institutional support for underrepresented AI researchers from Africa.

## ELLIS PhD Program

### Pitch
ELLIS represents the best of European machine learning research — and several ELLIS units are at the exact intersection of my interests: Amsterdam (VIS Lab, Asano/Gavves — self-supervised grounding), Oxford (Vedaldi — VGGT 3D geometry), ETH (Van Gool — multimodal vision), and Cambridge (Albanie — VLM probing).

### Why ELLIS Specifically
A pan-European program with joint supervision means I benefit from multiple mentors' perspectives — a theoretical grounding from one lab, experimental methodology from another, deployment context from a third. This is exactly the kind of interdisciplinary environment my research agenda requires.

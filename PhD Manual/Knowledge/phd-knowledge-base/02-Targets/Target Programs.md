# Target Programs

This note describes the kinds of programs, labs, and supervisors that fit Brian best.

It is not a checklist. It is a targeting map.

---

## What Brian is optimizing for

Brian is not simply looking for any funded PhD.
He is looking for an environment where his research identity can sharpen around:
- **visual grounding**,
- **multimodal perception**,
- **dynamic and temporal understanding**,
- **causal and trustworthy AI**,
- and applications where model failure actually matters.

The best targets are those that combine intellectual seriousness, technical depth, and strong supervision.

---

## Best-fit regions

### United Kingdom
A strong fit because of:
- world-class computer vision and scientific AI groups,
- high-quality supervision structures,
- strong research culture in grounding, geometry, and biomedical AI.

Best examples:
- **Oxford VGG / CDT ecosystem**
- **Cambridge-aligned vision and probing work**
- selected medically grounded VLM or trustworthy AI projects

### Europe
Europe is one of Brian's strongest fit regions because it offers:
- rigorous vision and multimodal groups,
- strong funding structures,
- excellent labs in causal ML, geometry, and scientific imaging,
- diverse paths through central programs and direct supervisor-led hiring.

Best examples:
- **ETH Zurich**
- **EPFL**
- **MCML / TUM ecosystem**
- **UvA VIS Lab**
- **ELLIS network**

### Canada
Canada fits when the lab is truly strong on representation learning, multimodal learning, or trustworthy ML.

Best example:
- **MILA**

### Australia
Australia is relevant when the fit is strong in vision and the supervision is high quality, though it is somewhat secondary to the strongest UK/Europe targets.

---

## Best-fit lab types

### 1. Vision and geometry labs
These are strong fits because Brian's long-term agenda is grounded in perception, not just text-driven intelligence.

Examples:
- Oxford VGG
- ETH CVG
- EPFL CVLab
- TUM / geometry and world-modeling groups

### 2. Multimodal and VLM reliability labs
These fit when the lab takes grounding, hallucination, or representation quality seriously.

Examples:
- VLM evaluation and probing groups
- multimodal representation learning labs
- perception-focused foundation model groups

### 3. Scientific and biomedical AI labs
These fit when the work remains technically deep and not purely domain-service oriented.

Examples:
- biomedical imaging groups
- scientific foundation model groups
- trustworthy medical VLM work

### 4. Causal and explainable ML groups
These are best when connected to representation learning, perception, or scientific reasoning rather than abstract causality alone.

---

## Supervisor preferences

Brian is best matched with supervisors who are:
- technically rigorous,
- active in publishing strong work,
- able to support open-ended but coherent research growth,
- interested in grounding, vision, multimodal learning, scientific AI, or trustworthy inference.

He is less well served by supervisors whose work is:
- very broad but weakly defined,
- heavily managerial or industrial without deep research ownership,
- only loosely connected to his core themes.

---

## Program quality filters

A target becomes more attractive when it has:
- clear supervisor alignment,
- credible funding,
- a strong publication culture,
- good methodological depth,
- and a real path toward becoming a distinctive researcher rather than a generic PhD student.

A target becomes less attractive when it is:
- too generic,
- unclear on supervision,
- weakly connected to perception or grounding,
- or mainly attractive because it is merely available.

## Bottom line

Brian should bias toward programs where he can become known for:
- grounded multimodal perception,
- trustworthy vision-language reasoning,
- dynamic or causal scene understanding,
- and technically serious AI for scientific or real-world settings.

That is the strategic center. Everything else should be judged relative to it.

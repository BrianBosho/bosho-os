# Research Direction

## Current Best-Fit Direction

The strongest repeated fit signal is:

Reliable multimodal AI with emphasis on interpretability, measurement, evaluation, and whether models are genuinely grounded in evidence rather than only producing correct-looking answers.

## Candidate Core Questions

- How do we tell whether a multimodal model is using visual evidence rather than shortcut correlations?
- How should reliability be measured beyond top-line correctness?
- Which metrics meaningfully track evidence use, grounding, and robustness?
- How can interpretability and measurement reinforce each other rather than remain separate threads?

## Working Domain Shape

- multimodal reliability
- visual grounding / evidence-use evaluation
- mechanistic interpretability
- benchmark and metric design
- real-world, low-resource, infrastructure-heavy domains

## Selection Rule

A direction is good if it:

- strengthens technical depth
- allows concrete artifacts
- has clear evaluation structure
- connects to broader AI safety / reliability concerns
- can support a coherent paper pipeline rather than isolated outputs

## Strategic Warning

Do not let the direction fragment into disconnected interesting topics. It should become a line of work, not a pile of curiosities.

# Research Dashboard

This note is the entry point for the research section of the vault.

The purpose of this section is to turn broad curiosity into increasingly concrete research objects:

> curiosity → informal question → formal question → topic/concept → reading list → people/labs → project brief → experiment/paper.

The research section should not become a museum of interests. It should help me compress intellectual ambition into tractable problems.

---

## Working Research Identity

I study the representational and information-theoretic foundations of visual grounding in multimodal models: why models succeed or fail to ground visual evidence, how visual information is transformed inside these systems, and what this reveals about the science of perception in learned systems.

Shorter version:

> I study how visual evidence flows through vision-language models, why grounding fails, and how to make multimodal systems more interpretable, reliable, and safe.

---

## Research Stack

### Layer 1: Publishable Core

Concrete work on:

- VLM grounding
- hallucination and visual fidelity
- VLM self-evaluation
- grounding calibration
- evidence attribution
- robustness benchmarks
- failure analysis

This is where papers come from.

### Layer 2: Scientific Abstraction

Deeper intellectual layer:

- semantic information
- information flow
- representation geometry
- evidence preservation/loss
- visual-to-language bottlenecks
- causal/mechanistic analysis of grounding

This is where the PhD identity becomes distinctive.

### Layer 3: Audacious Long-Term Vision

Long-term scientific ambition:

- theory of perceptual foundation models
- information-theoretic view of learned perception
- training objectives with formal guarantees around grounding/reliability
- contribution to the science of deep learning/intelligence

This is the north star, not the first-year deliverable.

## Current Priority

The immediate priority is not to solve the whole thesis.

The immediate priority is to produce a strong project brief and experimental plan around:

> Can VLMs reliably evaluate whether their outputs are grounded in visual evidence?

This project should function as the first practical testbed for the broader thesis arc.

---

## Research Development Rule

Every serious research idea must eventually answer five questions:

1. What is the phenomenon?
2. Why does it matter?
3. How can it be measured?
4. What mechanism might explain it?
5. What artifact can I produce this month?

If an idea cannot answer these, it remains a curiosity, not a project.

---

## Current Research Identity

Short form:

> I study how visual evidence flows through vision-language models, why grounding fails, and how to make multimodal systems more interpretable, reliable, and safe.

Long form:

> I study the representational and information-theoretic foundations of visual grounding in multimodal models: why models succeed or fail to ground visual evidence, how visual information is transformed inside these systems, and what this reveals about the science of perception in learned systems.

## Development Rule

Every serious research idea must eventually answer five questions:

1. What is the phenomenon?
2. Why does it matter?
3. How can it be measured?
4. What mechanism might explain it?
5. What artifact can I produce this month?

If an idea cannot answer these five, it stays in the curiosity layer.

## Weekly Research Loop

Each week should produce at least one:

- written question
- paper note
- synthesis memo
- experiment table

- project brief update
- message to a collaborator or mentor

Guiding question:

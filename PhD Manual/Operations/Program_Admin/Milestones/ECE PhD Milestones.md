# ECE PhD Milestones

Source: ECE PIT Catalog Handbook (2025–26), Dual-Degree Student Guide

## Overview

Eight milestones to complete for the ECE Ph.D.:

1. Coursework
2. Breadth Area Requirement
3. Qualifying Exam
4. Research (18990 every semester)
5. Teaching Internships (2 total)
6. Prospectus
7. Thesis
8. Dissertation Defense

## Coursework

**Required total (entering without an M.S.):**
- 8 ECE or related courses = 96 units total at CMU
- At least 6 of the 8 must be approved graduate-level courses
- Must also complete a **1-unit Introduction to Graduate Studies** in first semester

**Required total (entering with an M.S. from outside CMU's ECE):**
- 4 ECE or related courses = 48 units at CMU
- At least 3 of the 4 must be approved graduate-level courses
- Plus the 1-unit Intro to Graduate Studies

**Grade requirement:** B- or better in all courses counting toward the degree. Courses graded below B- do not count but still factor into cumulative QPA.

**QPA requirement:** Minimum 3.0 to graduate.

**Course load:** Typically 1–2 technical courses (12 units each) per semester + 18990 research units. More than 2 technical courses requires written advisor authorization (send to Greta Ruperto).

**Registration rule (CMU|Portugal):** At least 37 units first semester in Pittsburgh; 36 units every subsequent semester regardless of location.

**Professional development:** Up to 12 units total of 18-996, 18-997, 18-998 (3 units each) may count toward course requirements.

## Breadth Area Requirement

ECE has 7 breadth areas. Must take at least one graduate course from at least **three** of these areas:

- Algorithms, Complexity, and Programming Languages
- Applied Physics (Solid State/Magnetics/Fields)
- Artificial Intelligence, Robotics, and Control
- Circuits
- Computer Hardware Engineering
- **Signal Processing and Communications** ← primary fit for this research direction
- Software Systems and Computer Networking

Can petition to count one course from a previous degree toward one breadth area (then only 2 more required).

## Qualifying Exam

**Purpose:** Tests ability to think, speak, and write at a doctoral level. Tests research-area knowledge plus ECE fundamentals.

**Format:**
1. Submit **declaration + abstract + 3 technical papers** (due September or February)
   - Papers define the research area; ≤50 total pages; max 2 CMU-authored papers; max 1 co-authored by the student
   - Student rank-orders 7–10 ECE faculty as preferred examiners
2. Submit **review paper** (due October or March) — ≤4 pages, 2-column format; explains your area and the 3 papers
3. **Oral exam** (November or April) — 30-min conference-style talk + Q&A from a 3-person faculty committee

**Outcomes:** Pass (officially Ph.D. candidate) or fail (one retry allowed). Two failures = required to leave the program.

**Deadline (full-time students, entering Fall 2015 or later):**
- First attempt: no later than **4th semester** after admission
- Must pass: no later than **5th semester** after admission
- Summer semesters not counted
- Internship (fall/spring) pauses the clock by one semester

**Strategic note:** The 3 technical papers you select define your examination area — choose papers that anchor the VLM/interpretability/information-theoretic direction. This is also a good opportunity to force a research question compression early.

## Research — 18990 Reading & Research

- Required every fall and spring semester
- Section A: Pittsburgh; Section PP: Portugal (Pittsburgh-based CMU Portugal students still use Section A)
- Graded Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory by faculty advisor each semester (final semester gets a letter grade)

---

## Teaching Internship (TI)

- 2 TIs required over the program
- Each TI = enrollment in **18-991** (12 units); graded; must receive B or better
- Unpaid
- CMU|Portugal students: **one TI in Pittsburgh, one TI in Portugal**
- Apply through TAPS system: https://www.ece.cmu.edu/apps/taps/
- Contact Greta Ruperto to facilitate cross-department TI arrangements

---

## Prospectus

A formal presentation of the dissertation plan to the committee. Typically around Semester 7 (S4 in the table above). Details vary by advisor and committee.

---

## Dissertation Defense

Final milestone. Ph.D. degree certified after successful defense + submission of all paperwork + $0.00 tuition balance. Degree title: **Doctor of Philosophy in Electrical and Computer Engineering.**

## Key rules to know

- Cannot double-count a course used for a previous degree
- Transfer credit: maximum 1 graduate course (12 units) from another university
- Pass/no-pass and audit courses do NOT count toward degree requirements
- Leave of absence: max 2 years cumulative; milestones cannot be completed while on leave
- Probation triggered if semester or cumulative QPA drops below 3.0

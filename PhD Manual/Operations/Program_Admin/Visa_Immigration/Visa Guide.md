Source: Dual-Degree PhD Student Guide 2025–26

## US Period — F-1 / J-1 Visa (Carnegie Mellon)

International students at CMU are on either an **F-1** (student visa) or **J-1** (exchange visitor) visa, managed by the **Office of International Education (OIE)**.

**OIE contact:**
Office of International Education  
Carnegie Mellon University  
Cyert Hall, First Floor Atrium  
5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15213  
Phone: 412-268-5231  
Website: https://www.cmu.edu/oie

**Key F-1 rules to know:**
- F-1 students on a US residential campus (Pittsburgh) may take **maximum one online course per semester** that has no required in-person component
- US government regulations require F-1 and J-1 students to be enrolled in an **in-person degree program** with in-person coursework expectations while at CMU
- Any internship, CPT, or off-campus work authorization must go through OIE

**Before any internship or external program:**
Check with OIE for CPT/OPT authorization. Also check CMU Portugal and FCT rules. Do not confirm any internship before clearance.

## Portugal Period — Residence Study Visa

Non-EU citizens need a **Portuguese residence study visa** to study in Portugal.

**Process (general steps):**
1. Obtain acceptance letter / enrollment confirmation from FEUP
2. Apply for a **long-stay (Type D) student visa** at the Portuguese consulate/embassy in your country of residence
3. Upon arrival in Portugal, register for a **residence permit** with SEF (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras) or AIMA (the successor agency)

**Documents typically required (verify with consulate):**
- Valid passport
- Enrollment/acceptance letter from FEUP
- Proof of financial means (FCT scholarship letter)
- Housing proof (accommodation in Porto)
- Health insurance
- Criminal background check (apostilled)

**Timing:** Apply well in advance — Portuguese consulate appointments can have multi-week waits, especially in Kenya.

**CMU Portugal support:** The coordination office (info@cmuportugal.org) can provide guidance and documentation letters. Contact them as soon as the Portugal period start date is confirmed.

---

## Transition between periods

When transferring from CMU to Portugal or vice versa:
- Notify CMU Portugal coordination office in advance
- Transfers to CMU should align with the CMU Academic Calendar
- OIE must be notified of any status changes

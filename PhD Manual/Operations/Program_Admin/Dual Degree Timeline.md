# Dual Degree Timeline

## Confirmed structure (from admission letter + dual-degree student guide)

**Start date:** Fall 2026  
**Stipend (CMU period):** $43,800/year  
**Stipend (Portugal period):** €1,359.64/month (FCT scholarship — see [[04_Program_Admin/Funding/FCT Scholarship]])  
**Degrees awarded:** Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (CMU) + Ph.D. from Portuguese institution (FEUP)

**Residency split:**
- Up to **2 years at Carnegie Mellon University** (Pittsburgh)
- **3 years in Portugal** (FEUP / INESC TEC)

**Co-supervision model:**
- Two advisors: one at CMU, one at the Portuguese university — both highly involved
- A yearly **Work Plan** must be signed by student + both advisors, specifying research topics, host institutions, and tentative dates. Must be sent to FCT (when acquiring fellowship) and to the National Directors.
- Periodic conference call meetings between student and advisors are expected.

**First-semester registration:**
- CMU|Portugal students must register for **at least 37 units** in their first Pittsburgh semester
- **36 units** every semester before or afterward (regardless of location)
- Register for **18990 Reading & Research** every fall/spring: section A (Pittsburgh) or section PP (Portugal)

## Strategic implication

The PhD must be built around mobility.

Important research collaborations should be formed early enough at CMU that they can continue while in Portugal.

## Research strategy implications

- Build CMU feedback loops early.
- Establish FEUP alignment early.
- Choose projects that can survive location transitions.
- Maintain remote collaboration habits.
- Create a portable research workflow: code, data, writing, meetings, paper pipeline.

## Key process notes

**Reporting obligations:** Notify CMU Portugal coordination office (info@cmuportugal.org) when:
- Arriving at institution
- Submitting FCT documents
- Transferring between institutions
- Any address change (within 10 days)
- Academic problems, health issues

**Individual Development Plan (IDP):** CMU Portugal requires an IDP documenting professional/personal goals, facilitating advisor-advisee dialogue. Templates available from Carnegie Mellon.

**Acknowledgment:** All publications and presentations must acknowledge FCT and CMU Portugal Program. Required text (EN): "This work is co-financed by Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia (Portuguese Foundation for Science and Technology) through the Carnegie Mellon Portugal Program under the fellowship (insert FCT grant reference SFRH/BD/xxxxxx/20xx)."

**CITI RCR Training:** Required before starting research. Responsible Conduct of Research education (see ECE handbook).

# PhD Constitution

## Purpose

This PhD is not only a credential. It is a five-year life architecture across CMU, FEUP, Pittsburgh, Portugal, Europe, research communities, friendships, hobbies, travel, and intellectual growth.

The goal is not merely to survive the PhD. The goal is to become more alive, capable, connected, and formidable through it.

## Success definition

The PhD succeeds if I become a technically strong, well-networked, healthy, intellectually independent researcher with a coherent research identity in reliable and interpretable multimodal AI; produce a strong thesis and publications; build relationships across CMU, FEUP, and the broader AI safety / multimodal ecosystem; and actually live the experience of being in Pittsburgh, Portugal, and Europe rather than postponing life until after the PhD.

## Research identity

I study the representational and information-theoretic foundations of visual grounding in multimodal models: why models succeed or fail to ground visual evidence, how visual information is transformed inside these systems, and what this reveals about the science of perception in learned systems.

### Shorter version

I study how visual evidence flows through vision-language models, why grounding fails, and how to make multimodal systems more interpretable, reliable, and safe.

## Research stack

### Layer 1: publishable core

VLM grounding, hallucination, self-evaluation, visual evidence attribution, robustness, benchmarks, and interpretability methods.

This is where papers come from.

### Layer 2: scientific abstraction

Information flow, semantic evidence, representation geometry, signal-processing views of multimodal inference, and mechanistic analysis of visual perception in foundation models.

This is where intellectual identity comes from.

### Layer 3: audacious long-term vision

A theory of perceptual foundation models and a contribution to the science of deep learning/intelligence.

This is the north star, not the first-year deliverable.

## Non-negotiables

- I will not postpone life until after the PhD.
- I will not work in isolation for long periods.
- I will not allow vague ambition to replace concrete projects.
- I will not let advisor approval become my only source of direction.
- I will not let fear, urgency, or comparison dictate my decisions.
- I will not sacrifice sleep, health, friendship, travel, and joy as a default mode.
- I will not confuse being busy with becoming excellent.

## Known failure modes

### Diffusion

The state where I have many interesting ideas but no deliverable.

Countermeasure: [[02_Projects/Anti-Diffusion Protocol]].

### Isolation

The state where I think alone for too long and avoid exposing incomplete work.

Countermeasure: [[06_Life_OS/Anti-Isolation Protocol]].

### Survival mode

The state where I feel constantly behind and become reactive, myopic, anxious, and narrow.

Countermeasure: [[06_Life_OS/Anti-Burnout Protocol]].

### Life deferral

The state where I keep saying “after this deadline” until years pass.

## Aliveness standard

A good PhD week should include:

- one meaningful research artifact or movement
- one feedback interaction
- three exercise / walking / cycling actions
- one social or community touchpoint
- one block that is not in service of the PhD
- one moment of wonder, exploration, or rest without guilt

## Guiding rule

The PhD should make me more capable and more alive.

If the system produces papers but destroys health, curiosity, friendships, or joy, it is extraction.

If the system produces exploration but no artifacts, it is diffusion.

If the system produces busyness but no research identity, it is drift.

The target is:

> clear direction + concrete artifacts + strong feedback + serious relationships + protected aliveness.

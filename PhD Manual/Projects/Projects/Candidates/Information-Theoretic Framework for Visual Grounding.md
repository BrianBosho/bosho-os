# Information-Theoretic Framework for Visual Grounding

## Stage

Candidate.

This is not the active project until it has a measurable object and a minimal empirical test.

## One-sentence question

Can visual grounding failures in VLMs be modeled as failures of semantic information transfer from image evidence to language outputs?

## Why it matters

This could provide the deeper theoretical layer connecting grounding, hallucination, representation, and reliability.

## Connection to thesis identity

This is the bridge between practical VLM grounding work and the audacious information-theoretic ambition.

## Possible objects of study

- semantic information in visual tokens
- evidence preservation across layers
- information loss through projection
- image-to-text bottlenecks
- grounding as constrained information transfer
- hallucination as unsupported semantic commitment

## Current uncertainty

The terms “information,” “semantic evidence,” and “grounding” must be made mathematically or operationally precise.

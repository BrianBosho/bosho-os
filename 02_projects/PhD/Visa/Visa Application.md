## Visa checklist

1. **Check your I-20**  
    Make sure name, date of birth, program, start date, funding, and SEVIS ID are correct. Sign page 1.
    
2. **Pay SEVIS I-901 fee**  
    Use your SEVIS ID from the I-20. F-1 fee is **$350**. Print/save the receipt.  
    Official link: ([fmjfee.com](https://www.fmjfee.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "SEVIS fee"))
    
3. **Complete DS-160**  
    Choose the embassy/consulate where you will interview, likely **Nairobi** or **Kigali**, depending where you can attend. Print/save the DS-160 confirmation page.  
    Official link: ([CEAC](https://ceac.state.gov/genniv/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application (DS-160)"))
    
4. **Create visa appointment account**  
    Pay the MRV visa fee, schedule the F-1 interview, and choose passport delivery/pickup.  
    Official link: ([ustraveldocs.com](https://www.ustraveldocs.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "USTravelDocs"))
    
5. **Prepare interview documents**  
    Bring:
    
    - Passport, valid at least 6 months beyond intended entry
        
    - I-20, signed
        
    - SEVIS I-901 receipt
        
    - DS-160 confirmation
        
    - Appointment confirmation
        
    - Visa fee receipt
        
    - CMU admission/funding letter
        
    - CMU Portugal fellowship letter
        
    - Academic transcripts, CV, diploma
        
    - Evidence of ties/plans after study
        
    - Any research/advisor/program correspondence
        
6. **Attend interview**  
    Be ready to explain: why CMU, why this PhD, why dual-degree CMU–Portugal, your funding, and your long-term plan.
    
7. **After approval**  
    Track passport return through the appointment portal. Do not book non-refundable flights until visa is issued.
    
8. **Before travel**  
    You may usually enter the U.S. up to **30 days before** the I-20 program start date. On arrival, check your I-94 and complete CMU OIE SEVIS check-in/orientation; CMU says immigration check-in must be completed by the first day of classes .

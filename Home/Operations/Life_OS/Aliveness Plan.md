# Aliveness Plan

## Purpose

The PhD should not consume the life it is supposed to enrich.

This plan exists so that I do not wake up years later feeling used up, reactive, isolated, and regretful.

## Minimum aliveness standard

A good week includes:

- 3 exercise / walking / cycling actions
- 1 social activity or community touchpoint
- 1 protected non-work block
- 1 moment of exploration, beauty, or play
- 1 honest check on whether I am becoming reactive

## Protected time

### Wednesday evening

No PhD work unless there is a true deadline emergency.

### Saturday

Default life day.

Allowed:

- hiking
- cycling
- city exploration
- friends
- travel
- rest
- hobbies
- cooking
- reading for pleasure

Not allowed by default:

- guilt-work
- vague catching up
- endless paper reading because I feel behind

## Anti-regret questions

- Am I living in Pittsburgh or merely working there?
- Am I living in Portugal or merely surviving there?
- Am I using the dual-continent experience as a life opportunity?
- What memories am I actively creating?
- What would future me regret not doing?

## Life list seeds

### Pittsburgh

- explore neighborhoods
- regular walking/cycling routes
- museums, cafes, libraries
- hiking/outdoor trips
- CMU/Pitt social and academic events

### Portugal / Europe

- Porto life rituals
- Portuguese language and culture
- weekend trips
- conferences/summer schools combined with travel
- friendships outside the lab

## Weekly aliveness review

- Did I exercise?
- Did I see people?
- Did I experience the city/country?
- Did I protect non-work time?
- Did rest feel guilty or real?

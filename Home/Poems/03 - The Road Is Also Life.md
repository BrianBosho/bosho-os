# The Road Is Also Life

> [!note] Recitation
> When the future looks like an endless sequence of burdens; for remembering that the journey is already life.

Do not make the distant harbour  
judge each mile that brings you near.  
Do not hurry through the morning  
because the evening is unclear.

You are not travelling toward your life  
as though your life begins elsewhere.  
The road beneath your present feet  
is already yours to inhabit and care for.

Carry purpose, but not perpetual haste.  
Let purpose give direction, not chains.  
A traveller who sees only arrival  
has crossed the earth and gathered no terrain.

There will be work that lasts too long,  
doors delayed and seasons strange;  
plans that fail to hold their shape,  
and winds that make the vessel change.

Let these enlarge you, not accuse you.  
Learn the weather; mend the sail.  
A changed direction is not always wandering,  
nor is every delay a failure.

Gather friendship, skill, and wonder.  
Taste the day that bears the load.  
Look outward when the mind grows narrow;  
there is more to life than road.

No destination can restore  
the years you would not let yourself receive.  
No future triumph can return  
the ordinary evenings you refused to live.

Therefore travel with attention.  
Work, but let the burden cease.  
Keep some room for unearned laughter,  
for beauty, conversation, rest, and peace.

And when at last you reach the harbour,  
may you arrive not merely relieved,  
but rich in all the road has shown you—  
having lived, and not only achieved.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

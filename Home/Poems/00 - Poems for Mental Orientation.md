# Poems for Mental Orientation

A small cycle of original recitation poems for attention, work, fear, release, renewal, and the kleśas.

## The Cycle

- [[01 - Before the World|Before the World]] — Morning recitation; for establishing attention and state before the day begins.
- [[02 - The Appointed Hour|The Appointed Hour]] — Before work; for wholehearted action without making work a measure of the self.
- [[03 - The Road Is Also Life|The Road Is Also Life]] — When the future looks like an endless sequence of burdens; for remembering that the journey is already life.
- [[04 - When Fear Calls Itself Wisdom|When Fear Calls Itself Wisdom]] — For anxiety, over-analysis, and moments when threat-biased thinking presents itself as realism.
- [[05 - The Evening Gate|The Evening Gate]] — At the end of work; for releasing unfinished things and leaving duty behind.
- [[06 - Begin Again|Begin Again]] — After avoidance, delay, or failure; for returning without self-punishment.
- [[07 - The Five Shadows|The Five Shadows]] — A recognition poem based on Patañjali’s five kleśas: avidyā, asmitā, rāga, dveṣa, and abhiniveśa.

## Suggested Use

- **Morning:** [[01 - Before the World]]
- **Before work:** [[02 - The Appointed Hour]]
- **When life feels like only burden:** [[03 - The Road Is Also Life]]
- **When fear presents itself as realism:** [[04 - When Fear Calls Itself Wisdom]]
- **At the end of work:** [[05 - The Evening Gate]]
- **After avoidance or failure:** [[06 - Begin Again]]
- **For observing the kleśas:** [[07 - The Five Shadows]]

> [!tip]
> Recite the poem that matches the present state. The cycle is not a checklist.

# The Five Shadows

> [!note] Recitation
> A recognition poem based on Patañjali’s five kleśas: avidyā, asmitā, rāga, dveṣa, and abhiniveśa.

When the mind mistakes its weather  
for the measure of the sky,  
when the passing hour seems final  
and the frightened thought seems wise,

Know the first and deepest shadow:  
ignorance that veils the whole—  
taking what is brief for lasting,  
taking circumstance for soul.

Look again with patient vision.  
Name the fact, and leave the tale.  
Neither hope nor fear is knowledge;  
neither triumph proves you whole nor failure makes you fail.

When the voice within declares,  
“I am the worker, I am the deed;  
I am praised when work is fruitful,  
I am worthless when it does not succeed,”

Know the shadow born of “I”—  
the self confused with role and name.  
You are present before achievement;  
you remain when ends the game.

Let work alter skill and circumstance,  
let action shape the world outside;  
but do not ask the fruit of labour  
to complete what is complete inside.

When the mind pursues the pleasant,  
saying, “Only this will make me free;  
keep each doorway standing open,  
lose no future meant for me,”

Know attachment by its hunger,  
by the bargain it would make:  
“Give me certainty and promise,  
then the path I will undertake.”

Choose with care, but hold things lightly.  
Let the wanted come and go.  
Desire may point toward a direction;  
it need not command the road.

When the heart recoils from labour,  
from the message, page, or choice;  
when remembered pain surrounds it  
and avoidance finds a voice,

Know aversion by its shrinking,  
by the body drawn away.  
Do not wait to love the burden.  
Meet one portion of it today.

Let discomfort walk beside you.  
It need not become your guide.  
You may open what you fear to open  
with a steady hand and spacious mind.

When the future fills with danger,  
and the mind stands guard all night;  
when it calls its tension wisdom  
and its narrow watching sight,

Know the ancient grasp for safety,  
the fear that all may fall apart.  
Listen where it offers knowledge;  
do not give it rule of heart.

No choice can abolish uncertainty.  
No plan can promise loss will cease.  
Gather what is yours to gather;  
then act, and leave the rest in peace.

These five shadows colour seeing:  
ignorance, self, desire, disdain,  
and the clinging fear of endings  
that would hold the world in chains.

Do not hate them when they enter.  
Hatred is another snare.  
See them clearly, name them gently,  
and withdraw the food of care.

For what is watched is not the watcher.  
What is felt is not the whole.  
Thought and fear may pass through awareness;  
none can stain the seeing soul.

Therefore guard the gate of attention.  
Feed no shadow without end.  
What you practise gathers power;  
what you notice shapes the mind.

Choose discernment over blindness,  
witness over wounded pride,  
freedom over grasping hunger,  
willingness where fears reside.

Choose equanimity over fleeing,  
courage over constant guard.  
Let the mind become a clear lake,  
not a battlefield held hard.

Then meet the work that stands before you—  
not as judge, salvation, fate;  
but as one appointed movement  
given briefly to create.

Do the deed without becoming it.  
Take the road without the chain.  
Let the fruit fall where it chooses.  
Let success and loss remain.

When the hour has spent its measure,  
leave the unfinished where it lies.  
The world will wait beyond your watching.  
Morning does not need your eyes.

Again the shadows may assemble.  
Again the mind may lose the way.  
Again return to clear attention.  
Again begin the present day.

Not by never being troubled,  
not by mastering every sea,  
but by seeing every colouring  
and remembering: I am free.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

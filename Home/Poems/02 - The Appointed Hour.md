# The Appointed Hour

> [!note] Recitation
> Before work; for wholehearted action without making work a measure of the self.

Let work be work, and nothing more—  
no court in which your worth is tried,  
no altar where the self is offered,  
no proof that you deserve your life.

The hand may shape the world it meets,  
may study, mend, create, persuade;  
but no result can crown your being,  
nor can an error make it fade.

Before the praise, you were not lesser.  
After success, you are not more.  
The work may alter circumstance;  
it cannot touch the deeper core.

Then take the task without resentment,  
and give it neither fear nor throne.  
Bring it the strength the hour permits;  
you need not bring your whole life down.

Do not demand the perfect feeling.  
Do not make effort into war.  
Settle the body, name the action,  
and cross the simple threshold-door.

Attend to what is here before you.  
Let other duties wait their turn.  
Reality will give its answer;  
observe the answer, act, and learn.

And when the appointed hour is ended,  
mark where you stopped and leave it there.  
The unfinished world can wait in silence.  
Rise with empty hands and clearer air.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

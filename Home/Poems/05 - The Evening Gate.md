# The Evening Gate

> [!note] Recitation
> At the end of work; for releasing unfinished things and leaving duty behind.

There comes an hour when work must end,  
though work itself remains undone;  
for no life waits beyond completion,  
and no day owes you endless sun.

Mark where the hand has left the labour.  
Name what tomorrow may receive.  
Then close the page without apology,  
and grant yourself the right to leave.

The unfinished is not abandoned.  
The waiting task is not betrayed.  
A burden placed within its season  
need not be carried night and day.

Let the room release its questions.  
Let the shoulders lose their guard.  
You are not strengthened by remaining  
forever inwardly on watch.

The mind may summon one more problem,  
one more danger, one more thread.  
Answer gently: “Not in this hour.  
Tomorrow has not yet been led.”

Return your senses to the evening—  
water, food, the open air;  
music, silence, human voices,  
the ordinary world still there.

You are not merely one who labours,  
nor one who plans and bears and strives.  
The darkening sky does not demand you.  
The night asks only that you arrive.

So cross the gate with empty hands.  
Let duty sleep outside the door.  
What must be carried can be lifted  
when morning gives you strength once more.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

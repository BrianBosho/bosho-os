# Begin Again

> [!note] Recitation
> After avoidance, delay, or failure; for returning without self-punishment.

When you have lost the promised morning,  
when the work remained untouched,  
when plans once clear have turned against you  
and even hope feels like too much,

Do not build a court within you.  
Do not rehearse the charge all day.  
The hour already spent is finished;  
no punishment can make it stay.

Let error teach its proper lesson.  
Take the fact and leave the shame.  
A missed beginning is not destiny;  
a fallen step is not a name.

You are not healed by self-accusing,  
nor made more worthy through regret.  
The mind that kneels before the past  
cannot attend to what is present.

Begin smaller than your pride prefers.  
Begin later than the plan allowed.  
Begin without the perfect feeling.  
Begin unpraised and unavowed.

Open the page. Send the message.  
Stand, wash, walk, return, repair.  
Let one plain act become the evidence  
that agency is living there.

Do not promise transformation.  
Do not swear you will never stray.  
Keep faith with one appointed action,  
then keep it once again next day.

The path is made by many returns,  
not by a life that never bends.  
No failure has the final word  
while there remains the will to begin.

Rise without drama. Start without hatred.  
Let the next clear action be enough.  
The heart grows strong not by perfection,  
but by returning without giving itself up.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

# When Fear Calls Itself Wisdom

> [!note] Recitation
> For anxiety, over-analysis, and moments when threat-biased thinking presents itself as realism.

When fear calls itself wisdom,  
and caution borrows reason’s tongue,  
when every open road appears  
already lost before begun,

Do not obey the first conclusion,  
nor call the narrow vision whole.  
The frightened mind counts every danger  
and leaves uncounted every resource.

It names vigilance maturity,  
tension discipline, and dread foresight;  
it asks you to prepare forever  
before permitting you to live.

But wisdom neither hides the danger  
nor kneels before what might go wrong.  
It sees the risk, the strength, the opening,  
the help at hand, the road still long.

Ask what is fact and what is forecast.  
Ask what is useful now to know.  
Ask which fear can guide an action  
and which must simply come and go.

You need not conquer every future,  
nor seal each door against regret.  
No living choice is made with knowledge  
of every consequence unmet.

Gather enough. Then choose with courage.  
Let the uncertain remain so.  
A mind that waits for perfect safety  
will find no hour in which to go.

Fear may speak; you need not silence it.  
Only refuse to yield the throne.  
Let clear attention hear its warning,  
then make the final judgment on its own.

The world is neither kind nor hostile  
because the anxious mind insists.  
Look wider than the fear has looked,  
and meet the whole of what exists.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

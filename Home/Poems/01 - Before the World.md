# Before the World

> [!note] Recitation
> Morning recitation; for establishing attention and state before the day begins.

Before the world has made its claim,  
before the road is named too steep,  
the mind has chosen where to stand,  
what it will nourish, what it will keep.

For fear may dress in sober clothes  
and call its narrow vision fact;  
it counts the doors that may be closed,  
but not the hands prepared to act.

Look twice upon the field before you.  
The danger is not all that lies there:  
there also wait the open path,  
the offered hand, the strength to repair.

Attention is a little flame;  
do not expose it to every wind.  
Not all that matters must be carried,  
not every voice must enter the mind.

The state you practise gathers proof:  
fear finds a world that warrants fear;  
but steadiness reveals the step  
that agitation could not hear.

So meet the morning without armour.  
Let breath be deep and vision wide.  
Choose what deserves this hour of life,  
and leave the rest outside.

The mind goes first; the deed will follow.  
The deed will teach the mind anew.  
Begin from neither dread nor dreaming—  
begin awake, and see what is true.

---

## Practice Note

Read slowly enough for each stanza to register in the body. The purpose is not to force a mood, but to remember and rehearse a wiser orientation.

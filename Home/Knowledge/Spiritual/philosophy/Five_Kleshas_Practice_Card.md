# Five Kleśas — Practice Card

> Quick reference. When the mind feels blunted or overwhelmed, the practice is not to fight the kleśa but to notice: **"This thought is coloured."** That recognition already loosens it. Full version: [[Five_Kleshas]].

## The five, at a glance

| Kleśa | What it sounds like | The opposite | Catch-phrase to say |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Avidyā** (clouded seeing) | "Everything is on fire; delay = permanent failure." | **Viveka** — clear seeing | "I am seeing the entire future instead of the present fact." |
| **Asmitā** (identity-fusion) | "What am I even doing? I am the bottleneck." | Witnessing / non-identification | "I have turned this task into a statement about myself." |
| **Rāga** (attachment) | "I must keep every option and future open." | **Vairāgya** — non-attachment | "I am trying to retain every attractive possibility." |
| **Dveṣa** (aversion) | "I feel no eagerness to touch it." | Willingness / equanimity | "I am recoiling from the discomfort of this work." |
| **Abhiniveśa** (fear of loss) | "My future is damaged if I get this wrong." | Courage / accepting uncertainty | "My mind is treating uncertainty as a threat." |

## The loop (how they chain)

> **Rāga:** make the most of every opportunity →
> **Asmitā:** my ability proves who I am →
> **Abhiniveśa:** if I fail I damage my future →
> **Dveṣa:** contact with the work hurts, so I withdraw →
> **Avidyā:** separate problems collapse into one giant threat →
> **Blunting:** I feel nothing, because feeling everything would be intolerable.

## The target state — akliṣṭa (uncoloured)

Not excitement. Clear, sober, bounded, steady:

> Fedprop needs four mornings.
> The VLM team needs a plan and a first contribution.
> Keep the visa option open while I gather Portugal information.
> The app is not active this week.
> My exhaustion is a condition to work with, not a verdict about me.

# Mind OS — Map

## Function

A single map for the cluster of notes that describe one underlying state (overload + blunting during a high-stakes transition) from three different lenses, plus the structure that resolves it. When you feel scattered, start here and follow the lens that fits the moment.

## The same state, three lenses

| Lens | Note | What it gives you |
|---|---|---|
| Clinical / ACT–CBT | [[Functional_Formulation]] | Names the mechanism (avoidance, perfectionism, fusion…) and its antidote. |
| Yogic / Patañjali | [[Five_Kleshas]] · [[Five_Kleshas_Practice_Card]] | Names the *colouring* (rāga, dveṣa, asmitā…) and how to uncolour it. |
| Attention / agency | [[Mind_Attention_Environment]] | Explains how state, attention, and belief produce behaviour — and how to shift them. |

They are not competing theories. They describe the same loop:

> **Rāga / opportunity-maximizing** → I must secure every future.
> **Asmitā / identity-fusion** → my output proves who I am.
> **Abhiniveśa / anticipatory anxiety** → if I fail I damage my future.
> **Dveṣa / task aversiveness** → contact with the work hurts, so I withdraw.
> **Avidyā / cognitive overload** → distinct problems collapse into one enormous threat.
> **Blunting** → I feel nothing, because feeling everything would be intolerable.

## From insight to action

Insight alone doesn't move the state — structure does. The shared resolution is [[Default_Week_Architecture]]: recurring containers that decide the *category* so the first five minutes only have to decide the *output*.

Three working rules that all three lenses converge on:

1. **Bounded contact, not excitement.** Open the avoided thing, work one 90-minute unit, stop at a defined point. Willingness > motivation.
2. **Separate the task from the self.** "The paper is delayed" — not "I am a delayed person." Behavioural accountability over global judgment.
3. **Evidence over omens.** Rebuild self-trust with small kept commitments, reviewed weekly — not with better plans.

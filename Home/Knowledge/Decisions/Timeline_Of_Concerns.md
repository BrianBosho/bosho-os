# Timeline Of Concerns

This page is the temporal view of the current corpus. It is still provisional and should be refined as source material is consolidated.

## Phase 1: Pressure Accumulation

- Several years of continuous work pressure, cognitive load, and future-orientation.
- Growing fatigue, burnout risk, and difficulty experiencing true recovery.
- Work increasingly becomes identity, structure, and emotional regulation.

## Phase 2: Recognition Of The Real Bottleneck

- Repeated realization that the issue is not mainly intelligence or ambition.
- Stronger diagnosis around self-trust erosion, unstable routines, and pressure loops.
- More explicit distinction between rest and real recovery.

## Phase 3: Pre-PhD Reframing

- The coming PhD becomes the main horizon.
- The question shifts from "can I push harder?" to "how do I enter the next stage sustainably and coherently?"
- Research identity, technical depth, and collaboration structure become central.

## Phase 4: Coherence Building

- Attempts to design a personal operating system.
- More explicit systems for deep work, sleep, closure, and research execution.
- Stronger separation of structure problems from root-analysis problems.

## Phase 5: Spiritual Integration

- Buddhist, Vipassana, and Dhammapada material become integrated into the same worldview.
- The recurring aim becomes excellence without identity-fusion to outcomes.
- Heedfulness, detachment, and non-negligence become practical standards rather than only ideals.

## What This Timeline Suggests

- The vault is documenting a transition from pressure-driven striving toward a more coherent research and life architecture.
- The next stage should convert this narrative arc into decision records and milestone pages.

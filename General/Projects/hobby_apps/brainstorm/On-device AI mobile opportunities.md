## The edge AI technical baseline

Modern mobile devices can already run meaningful open-weight AI locally. On the text side, the most practical open-weight options for broad mobile distribution are **Gemma 4 E2B/E4B**, **Llama 3.2 1B/3B**, **Phi-3 Mini 3.8B**, and **Qwen3 4B**. On the audio side, **Whisper remains the safe default**, especially through `whisper.cpp`, which supports iOS and Android, integer quantisation, VAD, and Core ML acceleration; `whisper.cpp` reports more than a **3├Ś** encoder speed-up on Apple Silicon when using Core ML. For vision and OCR, the most production-ready local stack is still **PaddleOCR mobile** for text extraction and document structure, with a small VLM layered on only where OCR alone is insufficient. ## What actually fits on a mid-tier phone

A reasonable 2026 ÔÇťmid-tier modern Android phoneÔÇŁ reference is still roughly **8 GB RAM and 128 GB storage**, which lines up with devices such as SamsungÔÇÖs Galaxy A56 5G and GoogleÔÇÖs Pixel 8a 128 GB configuration. That is enough for a meaningful local AI product, but only if the workload is designed around bursts, not around a continuously running general assistant. ţłÇciteţłéturn20search3ţłéturn20search10ţłü

The easiest wins are OCR-heavy utilities. A full PaddleOCR mobile stack is measured in **megabytes**, not gigabytes, and is therefore cheap in both storage and memory relative to language models. This is why document scanning, receipt parsing, PDF searchability, and local redaction are excellent indie opportunities: they feel magical to users, but the core inference path is far lighter than shipping a big all-purpose model. ţłÇciteţłéturn22view2ţłéturn32view0ţłü

The next safe tier is streaming or burst speech recognition. Whisper tiny/base and Moonshine tiny/base are realistic on mainstream 8 GB phones; Whisper small can still work, but you need to be much more careful about thermal budget, background behaviour, and how much else is resident in memory. Anything in the Whisper medium-and-above range is a poor match for the median handset unless the app is explicitly prosumer and users accept slower, hotter, or plug-in workflows. ţłÇciteţłéturn24search0ţłéturn22view7ţłéturn26search4ţłü

For text generation or structured extraction, the practical mainstream envelope is **1B to about 3ÔÇô4B parameters, quantised**. The evidence is straightforward from real mobile-friendly checkpoints: Llama 3.2 3B Q4 is about **1.92 GB**, Phi-3 Mini Q4 about **2.2 GB**, and Qwen3 4B Q4_K_M about **2.5 GB**. On an 8 GB phone, that is feasible, but it is already the upper zone for reliable coexistence with camera pipelines, audio recording, and a normal app shell. My practical planning rule would be: **treat 1BÔÇô3B as broad-market, 4B as upper-mid-tier, and anything heavier as premium-only**. That last sentence is an inference from the published model sizes and mobile memory realities rather than a vendor guarantee. ţłÇciteţłéturn16search7ţłéturn17search0ţłéturn16search11ţłéturn22view5ţłéturn22view4ţłü

Battery and thermal limits matter more than headline compatibility. Apple and Android both provide first-party power profiling tools, which is a quiet but important reminder that sustained inference can materially heat devices and hit battery rails. In product terms, that means the winning pattern is **capture first, infer second**: use VAD for speech, batch OCR into document-level passes, run summarisation after capture rather than continuously, and keep camera/VLM analysis snapshot-driven unless you are on premium silicon. WhisperÔÇÖs Core ML speed-up and LiteRTÔÇÖs GPU/NPU path both help, but they do not remove the fundamental thermal constraints of long-running local inference. ţłÇciteţłéturn19search16ţłéturn19search17ţłéturn22view7ţłéturn22view3ţłü

## What users hate in the current market

In scanner and OCR apps, the pattern is simple: users love the core utility but resent subscriptions, cloud-first behaviour, and overreach. CamScanner is enormous at **500M+** downloads and top-grossing in productivity, but its listing shows subscriptions billed weekly through annually, and one surfaced review explicitly calls the trial-to-annual conversion ÔÇťvery predatoryÔÇŁ. Its data-safety section says it may collect personal and financial information, while a public consumer review describes sensitive documents being uploaded to the cloud without the user realising it. Adobe Scan is similarly mass-market and strong, but it auto-saves scans to Adobe Document Cloud and uses premium gating for more intensive OCR workflows. Meanwhile, smaller privacy-first alternatives like Genius Scan, vFlat, and MakeACopy lean hard into **on-device processing, no sign-in, no ads, no tracking**, which tells you exactly where the trust deficiency is in the mainstream market. ţłÇciteţłéturn9view0ţłéturn8search20ţłéturn12view4ţłéturn10search3ţłéturn12view5ţłéturn12view6ţłéturn32view0ţłü

In transcription and AI note-taking, the biggest complaints are **internet dependence, hidden or aggressive paywalls, slow upload pipelines, and reliability failures after recording**. OtterÔÇÖs own Play listing says its real-time transcription is for use **when online**. Notta has a surfaced complaint saying the app has no functional free version once a recording is made and pushes a **$14/month** or **$100/year** plan. Other note-taker listings surface exactly the kind of failure mode a local-first app can defeat: long uploads, deleted recordings, lost notes, crashes when opening meetings, and unclear billing. ## Open questions and limitations

This report is strongest on **technical feasibility, model fit, and visible product gaps**, and weaker on **exact App Store / Google Play keyword volumes**, because first-party stores do not expose robust public volume data. The competitor-complaint section is based on surfaced public reviews and store listings rather than a full corpus scrape of every review.

The main product risk is not whether on-device AI works at all; it does. The main risk is choosing a concept that needs too much continuous inference for too little user value. The winning indie products here will be the ones that keep local inference **narrow, bursty, and operationally simple**.

# Paragraph Module: Why Africa / Long-term Mission

## Version A — Full (for fellowships and longer letters)
My long-term ambition extends beyond technical contributions. I want to establish myself as a researcher who advances the foundations of robust AI while creating pathways for the next generation of scientists, particularly in Africa. My commitment to this is grounded in action, not aspiration: as Local Chair for the African Computer Vision Summer School (ACVSS 2025) in Kigali, I helped coordinate a 10-day event connecting top African talent with global leaders in computer vision. Mentoring students at CMU-Africa has reinforced my belief that cutting-edge research and capacity building are mutually reinforcing — the quality of both improves when more people participate.

## Version B — Integrated (for letters where this is secondary)
My research questions are not disconnected from context: the failure modes I study daily arise from deploying VLMs in Kigali's traffic — low-resource, non-Western, underrepresented in training data. Building AI that is reliable in these contexts is important science, not just applied work. And building the capacity for African researchers to pursue such questions at the frontier is part of the same project.

## Version C — Brief (2 sentences, for space-limited applications)
Beyond technical research, I am committed to building AI research capacity in Africa — a commitment reflected in my role as Local Chair of ACVSS 2025. I see rigorous research and equitable participation as mutually reinforcing goals.

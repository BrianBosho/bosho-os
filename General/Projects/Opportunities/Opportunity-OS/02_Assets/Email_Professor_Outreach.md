# Email — Professor Outreach Template

> Agents: keep the final email under 250 words. Every sentence must earn its place.
> Customize [ALL BRACKETED SECTIONS] — do not send the template.
> Attach: CV. Optionally: 1-page research summary.

---

**Subject:** Prospective PhD Student — [YOUR RESEARCH TOPIC] / [YOUR NAME]

---

Dear Prof. [LAST NAME],

[HOOK — 1-2 sentences connecting their specific work to yours. Name a paper, a result, or a specific research question from their lab. Show you have read their work, not just their website.]

Example: "Your work on [VQA-CP / specific paper] directly challenges the field to move beyond accuracy toward models that cannot exploit language priors — which is exactly the problem my research aims to extend with intrinsic diagnostic tools."

[YOUR RESEARCH — 2-3 sentences. What is the specific question you are working on? What is your proposed approach? What early results or ideas do you have? Be concrete — this is not a vague interest statement.]

Example: "I am developing a Visual Groundedness Toolkit to measure, from internal model states, whether a VLM's predictions are causally informed by visual input or driven by language priors. My approach combines information-theoretic diagnostics (Visual Information Gain) with spectral analysis of token-graph Laplacians — building on spectral tools I used to analyze propagation in federated GNNs (FedProp, under review at IEEE ICC)."

[YOUR CREDENTIALS — 2 sentences. The strongest 2 facts from your profile. No more.]

Example: "I completed my M.S. in Engineering AI at CMU-Africa (GPA 3.97, Valedictorian) and am currently a Research Associate working on VFM evaluation for urban mobility in Kigali."

[SPECIFIC QUESTION OR ASK — 1 sentence. Do not ask "are you accepting students?" — that wastes their time. Ask something that shows genuine engagement.]

Example: "I would welcome your thoughts on whether my proposed use of spectral Laplacian analysis on attention token graphs aligns with directions your lab is currently exploring."

Best regards,
Brian Kipkirui
[Email] | [GitHub] | [Personal site]

---

## Before Sending Checklist

- [ ] Email is under 250 words
- [ ] Named a specific paper or result of theirs
- [ ] Research description is concrete (method + question, not vague interest)
- [ ] Credentials section is exactly 2 sentences, strongest facts only
- [ ] Subject line includes your research topic and your name
- [ ] CV attached (correct version for research context)
- [ ] No typos in professor's name or institution
- [ ] Sent at a reasonable time (not 3am their timezone)

## Timing Notes

- Best time to send: Tuesday–Thursday, morning target timezone
- Follow up: after 2 weeks if no response, once only
- Do not email during major conference deadlines (NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML submission periods)

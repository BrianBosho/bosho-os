# Bio — Medium (150 words)

Brian Kipkirui is a Research Associate at Carnegie Mellon University Africa, where he studies vision foundation models for urban mobility analysis in low-resource environments. He holds an M.S. in Engineering Artificial Intelligence from CMU-Africa (GPA 3.97, Valedictorian, 2025) and a dual B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from JKUAT, Kenya.

His research centers on visual groundedness in multimodal foundation models — developing information-theoretic diagnostics and spectral manifold analysis tools to measure when vision-language models rely on language priors rather than genuine visual evidence. His prior work includes FedProp, a communication-efficient federated GNN algorithm grounded in spectral graph theory, and deep learning pipelines for 3D Cryo-Electron Tomography at CMU's Xulab.

Brian served as Local Chair for the African Computer Vision Summer School (ACVSS) 2025 in Kigali. He is seeking a PhD position to advance robust multimodal perception research.

# Paragraph Module: Why AI

## Version A — Engineering path (formal, for academic letters)
My path into machine learning began with an undergraduate degree in Electrical Engineering, which gave me a strong foundation in signals, systems, and mathematics. After graduating, I worked in industry and completed a postgraduate certification in data science, during which AI moved from a marginal curiosity to a serious intellectual interest. Encountering advances like AlphaFold, GPT models, and autonomous systems convinced me that learning-based methods would drive the next wave of scientific and engineering progress — and that my engineering foundation had prepared me to contribute to that effort.

## Version B — Problem-driven (sharper, for research-focused letters)
I am driven by the conviction that artificial intelligence will become one of humanity's primary tools for advancing critical domains — medicine, climate, infrastructure — but only if we can trust it. Foundation models already exhibit striking capabilities, yet they remain dangerously unreliable: confident in their outputs while quietly ignoring or hallucinating crucial evidence. That gap between transformative promise and actual reliability is what motivates my research.

## Version C — Short (for space-limited applications, 2 sentences)
My research is motivated by a simple observation: today's best vision-language models can achieve high benchmark accuracy while completely ignoring the visual evidence they are supposed to process. Closing that gap — ensuring models are not just right, but right for the right reason — is the central problem I want to work on.

## Visa Requirements by Region

### Canada
- **PhD path:** Student permit tied to university admission — standard, well-established
- **Work after PhD:** Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) available
- **Status for Mila:** Application submitted — if admitted, student permit is the path
- **Effort:** Medium — needs university offer letter first

### European Union
- **Work visa:** EU Blue Card — requires job offer + salary threshold (varies by country)
  - Germany: ~45,000 EUR/year threshold (lower for shortage occupations)
  - Netherlands: ~46,000 EUR/year (lower for under-30 knowledge migrants)
  - France, Belgium, others: similar Blue Card framework
- **PhD path:** Student visa per country — usually tied to university enrollment + scholarship
  - Germany: funded PhD = residence permit, manageable
  - Netherlands: funded PhD = residence permit, manageable
  - France: Carte de séjour étudiant, manageable with INRIA/CNRS funding
- **Key point:** Visa sponsorship must be explicitly confirmed by employer/institution
- **Self-sponsorship:** Not available — needs employer or university

### United Kingdom
- **Skilled Worker Visa:** Needs sponsor employer on Home Office register + salary floor
- **Global Talent Visa:** Requires endorsement from Royal Academy of Engineering or similar body — realistically available post-PhD with strong research profile
- **PhD/Student path:** Tier 4 student visa — standard with university offer
- **Effort:** High for jobs without PhD; Medium for PhD route

### United States
- **Work:** H-1B lottery system — very difficult, not reliable path
- **PhD:** F-1 student visa — possible but USA deprioritized in preferences
- **Verdict:** Do not prioritize US jobs. US PhDs acceptable if extraordinary lab.

### Kenya
- **Right to work:** Full — Kenyan citizen
- **No visa needed**
- **Remote work for foreign companies:** Legal while in Kenya — no issue

### Rwanda
- **Current residence:** Already here
- **EAC (East African Community):** Free movement across Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, South Sudan — can work in EAC countries

## Countries Where Visa-Sponsored Jobs Are Most Realistic

Ranked by realistic chance of visa sponsorship for ML/AI roles:

1. **Germany** — Blue Card program, many tech companies sponsor, active ML scene
2. **Netherlands** — Highly International Migration (HIGHLY SKILLED MIGRANT) permit, very accessible
3. **France** — Tech sector active, INRIA/CNRS academics commonly sponsor
4. **Belgium** — EU Blue Card, Brussels AI hub
5. **Sweden, Denmark** — Good sponsorship culture, AI research labs
6. **UK** — Skilled Worker path, but more complex; better post-PhD

## Flags for Agents

- If a job says "EU citizens only" or "no sponsorship" → mark infeasible, skip
- If a job says "will consider visa sponsorship" → mark feasible, flag for confirmation
- If a PhD is fully funded → student visa almost always manageable, mark feasible
- If remote and company is OK hiring internationally → no visa issue, mark feasible
- Always check: does the role/country have a realistic visa pathway? If unclear → flag for human review

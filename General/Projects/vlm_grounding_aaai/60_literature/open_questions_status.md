## Closed Answers to Core Questions

### CQ1 — The contrastive-decoding boundary
**Answer:** Contrastive decoding methods like VCD ([2311.16922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16922)) and M3ID ([2403.14003](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14003)) actively subtract logit distributions generated by distorted inputs to *steer* generation at decoding time. In contrast, our real-vs-null counterfactual is a passive *measurement* instrument that uses a static, content-free baseline (dataset-mean patch) to evaluate the model's visual commitment per token. Furthermore, we separate ourselves from simple logit subtraction by introducing (a) a spatial concept profile that maps *where* the image mattered, and (b) a causal patch-ablation check of *whether* those patches are necessary, neither of which has a decoding-steering analogue.

### CQ2 — Self-evaluation landscape
**Answer:** LLM self-evaluation traditionally measures verbalized confidence or semantic consistency of text outputs (e.g. P(True) [2207.05221](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.05221) and Semantic Entropy [2302.09664](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.09664)). In the visual domain, VAUQ ([2602.21054](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21054)) estimates VLM answer correctness via a scalar image-dependence score computed at the output level. Our approach occupies a new axis of "intrinsic visual self-evaluation" by looking under the hood at token-level internal representations, mapping spatial dependence, and validating it causally. This allows us to identify "right for the wrong reason" cases where output confidence is high but visual grounding is low.

### CQ3 — Internal grounding signals
**Answer:** Prior hallucination detectors extract internal signals using middle-layer contextual embeddings (Beyond Logit Lens [2411.19187](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.19187)), attention activations (What's in the Image [2411.17491](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17491)), or embedding covariance eigenvalues (INSIDE's EigenScore [2402.03744](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03744)). We borrow the insight that dense internal layers (like LLava's Layer-32) hold raw grounding signals that are lost during token decoding. However, we uniquely isolate visual attribution from general model uncertainty by subtracting the dataset-mean null state, which removes the high anisotropy floor of internal representations.

### CQ4 — Causal necessity and patch attribution
**Answer:** Prior works evaluate visual attribution using cross-modal attention maps (F-LMM [2406.05821](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.05821)), attention tracking ([2411.17491](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17491)), or textual explanation rationales (ERASER [1911.03429](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.03429)). In NLP, faithfulness is evaluated by removing rationales and measuring performance degradation. To avoid test-time distribution shifts identified by deep learning interpretability benchmarks like ROAR ([1806.10758](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.10758)), our causal track systematically ablates targeted top-k visual patches and compares them against matched random patch ablations, confirming that performance drops are specifically driven by the necessity of the visual content.

### CQ5 — Per-token claim vs. POPE
**Answer:** Evaluation of token-level grounding in generation tasks traditionally relies on caption-matching metrics like CHAIR ([1809.02156](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.02156)) or VLM-based fact-verification like FaithScore ([2311.01477](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.01477)). Although our quantitative results on POPE ([2305.10355](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10355)) evaluate yes/no answer tokens, the underlying instrument computes grounding scores for every single token generated by the VLM. We justify teacher forcing during evaluation as a standard way to isolate grounding from generation-time search bias. To bridge the gap, we define our method as an "inference-time token-level grounding instrument," illustrating its use qualitatively on free-form captions while relying on POPE for stable quantitative evaluation.

### CQ6 — Dataset completeness and partial labels
**Answer:** Open-ended generation benchmarks like CHAIR and AMBER ([2311.07397](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.07397)) are vulnerable to the "partial-labeling trap," where correct model descriptions of unannotated objects are penalized as false hallucinations. We utilize POPE's closed, polling-based binary format precisely because it bypasses this labeling bottleneck by asking targeted queries. For open-ended grounding validation, we must use exhaustively annotated datasets constructed on top of complete segmentations, such as Panoptic Narrative Grounding ([2109.04988](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.04988)), where "not in labels" reliably signifies absence in the image.

### CQ7 — Differentiation Matrix

The matrix proves our unique positioning: we are the only work that combines **self-evaluation** and **visual grounding** with **internal-state signals**, **spatial localization**, and **causal validation** under a training-free measurement framework.

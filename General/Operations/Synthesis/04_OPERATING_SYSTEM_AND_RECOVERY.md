# Operating System And Recovery

## First Principles

- Reliability before output.
- Recovery before optimization.
- Defaults before willpower.
- Closure before expansion.
- One real block of depth is worth more than a day of agitated pseudo-work.

## Non-Negotiable Base

- Fix sleep first.
- Use early hours for the highest-value thinking.
- Decide the first task the night before.
- Keep the first work block concrete and bounded.
- Protect transitions: ending work matters almost as much as starting it.

## Recovery Model

Recovery is not absence of work. It is active restoration of capacity.

Required layers:

- Physical: sleep, food, movement, sunlight, lower bodily tension.
- Cognitive: periods where nothing is being optimized or solved.
- Emotional: reduce chronic urgency and self-monitoring.
- Existential: stop asking each day to justify the whole life.

## Practical Rules

- Rebuild self-trust through repeated small proofs.
- If a plan feels self-deceptive after two honest minutes, shrink it.
- Make sitting and starting non-decision-based.
- Do not redesign the entire life when basic rhythm is unstable.
- Use defined artifacts and defined end conditions.
- Treat recovery bandwidth as a finite resource that must be budgeted.

## Weekly Operating Shape

- One main research direction per period, not many.
- One defined deep-work block per day.
- One collaboration move per week.
- One review point for open loops and next actions.
- One protected recovery half-day or full day that is not secretly optimization.

## Canonical One-Liners

- "Sleep early -> wake early -> think before the world interferes."
- "If something can be fixed by structure, don't analyze it."
- "I earn the right to analyze by first executing."
- "Structure is internal safety, not productivity theater."

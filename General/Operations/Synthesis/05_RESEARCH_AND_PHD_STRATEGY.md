# Research And PhD Strategy

## Strategic Frame

The PhD should be treated as a five-year authority engine, not a passive credential.

Desired outcome:

- become a credible researcher-builder
- build real technical depth
- produce artifacts that compound into optionality
- leave the PhD with stronger choices, not narrower ones

## Research Identity

Most recurring fit signals point toward:

- reliable multimodal AI
- mechanistic interpretability
- measurement and evaluation
- visual grounding / evidence-use questions
- real-world, low-resource, infrastructure-heavy application domains

## Entry Conditions For A Good PhD

- strengthens technical rigor
- increases credibility
- expands network and collaborators
- allows concrete artifact production
- preserves future optionality across academia, labs, and institution-building

## Technical Development Priorities

- strong Python and ML research engineering habits
- reproducibility and clean repos
- experiment design and evaluation discipline
- config, logging, and environment hygiene
- paper reimplementation and modular implementation skill
- ability to produce small memos, figures, evaluations, and benchmark-style artifacts

## Collaboration Strategy

Isolation is a real risk and should be treated as a system problem.

Working rules:

- no serious project should stay solo for more than 3-4 weeks
- maintain anchor relationships
- target a small number of active collaborators rather than many weak plans
- ask people to react to concrete artifacts, not vague aspirations
- keep a simple collaboration board with status and next action

## Anti-Failure Rules

- do not wait passively for advisor structure
- do not publish disconnected papers without a coherent line
- do not confuse intellectual taste with execution readiness
- do not let engineering weakness become a hidden ceiling
- do not let health instability make research feel like dread
